FAQS

1.What is integrated Medicine ?

It is the amalgamation of two or more systems of healing or treatment. Integrated medicine is health care that uses all appropriate therapeutic approaches - Conventional & non - mainstream - within a framework that focuses on health, that takes account of the whole person (body, mind, and spirit), including all aspects of lifestyle.

2.What are the health issues / ailments that are treated through integrated medicine?

We offer medical advice & treatment for all health issues & for all age groups including both sets or genders .Children, Adolescents, and the elderly can all benefit from its treatment.

3.Do you refer your patients to other speciality hospitals or clinics?

We provide the best primary care to our patients, but there are some patients with severe complicated issues that require the attention of other super specialists. In such cases, we provide a free referral to the appropriate doctors who are a part of our team.

4.What is the line of treatment / line of management ?

Diagnosis at the time of consultation is the centrepiece for deciding the line of treatment. Primarily, patients are guided thoroughly about life style changes and medication is prescribed only if considered necessary. Adjuvants of treatments also include physical therapy, yoga, balanced diet, acupressure / acupuncture and other alternative therapies.

5.Do you have in - patient facilities ?

We don't have in - patient facility and only offer treatment to out - patients.

6.Why wellness Homeo Clinics International ?

At our clinics we treat our patients through a holistic approach in an integrated way. We believe in keeping it simple & rely on our analytic skills to identify the cause of ailment & then plan the treatment. The time tested & reliable method of preparing a detailed history & conducting basic physical examination is followed assiduously & has a distinct advantage over the current practice of suggesting a battery of tests to find the source of the problem. We resort to investigations only to conform a positive possible diagnosis or to rule out any lethal conditions where a physical examination is contradictory or doesn't reveal the true nature of the problem. In today's scenario it is easy to confuse medicare & healthcare and often it is found that both are projected as the same. At our clinics we take the effort to distinguish between the two to make sure that patients are not confused by varying methodologies and complicated jargon
